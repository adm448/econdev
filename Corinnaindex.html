Female Internet access across the world: #menot


Corinna Fehst

Motivation


Like in so many other areas of life, women do not yet have equal rights to men when it comes to Internet access. Today, women in many countries of the world still face disadvantages in Internet Access compared to their male fellow citizens - sometimes to a significant degree.

The interactive map below visualizes this Female Internet Access Gap. It seeks to create transparency and to thereby motivate change.


The Female Internet Access Gap - Methodology


For each country where reliable data is available, the map below displays the percentage point (% pt) difference between male and female Internet access rates, along with the total male and female Internet access rate for the given country.

As such, there are two main outcomes for a country:

  • Female Internet Access Gap : Countries where women have lower Internet access than men are coloured in a shade of red or orange, depending on the size of the gap

  • No Female Internet Access Gap Countries where women have equal or higher Internet access than men are highlighted in shades of green, depending on how much larger female access is.

Example: if female Internet penetration in Country X is 50% (meaning 50% of women in Country X have access to the Internet) and male Internet penetration is 60%, then the Female Internet Access Gap is -10 % pts, and Country X will be colored in light red.
